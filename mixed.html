OneVote

Mixed

What is it?


Mixed Voting is a Voting System where
a person votes two times; one for their
prefered candidate, and one for their
prefered party.

Versions:


Mixed Member Proportional (MMP)
Additional Member System (AMS)


Major Countries that use this system:


New Zealand
Germany
Bolivia

How to Vote

Step 1

Take a Ballot and go into a vacant Voting Booth.

Step 2

Look at your ballot. On the left will be a list of parties. On the right will be a list of names of the candidates running for your electorate.

Step 3

On the left, place a single tick next to the party you want to vote for. On the right, place a single tick in the box next to the name of the candidate you want to vote for.

Step 4

Make sure to only tick one candidate and one party. If you make a mistake, take your ‘spoiled’ ballot back to an election official and ask for a new one.

Step 5

Once you have marked your ballot, fold your ballot paper in half, exit the booth, and drop it into the ballot box.
