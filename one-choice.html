OneVote

One Choice

What is it?


One Choice Voting (also called Single
Choice Voting) is a Voting System where a
person votes for One Candidate on a
ballot.

Versions:


First Preference Plurality (FPP)
Proportional Representation (PR)


Major Countries that use this system:


United Kingdom
United States
Canada

How to Vote

Step 1

Take a Ballot and go into a
vacant Voting Booth

Step 2

Look at your ballot.
On it will be a list of names of
the candidates running for your electorate/division.

Step 3

Place a single tick/cross
in the box next to the name of the
candidate you want to vote for.

Step 4

Make sure to only tick one candidate.
If you make a mistake, take your
‘spoiled’ ballot back to an election
official and ask for a new one.

Step 5

Once you have marked your ballot, fold your ballot paper in half, exit the booth, and drop it into  into the ballot box.
