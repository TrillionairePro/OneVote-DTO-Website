OneVote

Ranked

What is it?


One Choice Voting is a Voting System where
a person ranks candidates in their prefered
order on a ballot.

Versions:


Instant-Runoff Voting (IRV)
Single Transferable Vote (STV)


Major Countries that use this system:


Australia
Ireland
Malta

How to Vote

Step 1

Take a Ballot and go into a
vacant Voting Booth

Step 2

Look at your ballot.
On it will be a list of names of
the candidates running for your electorate/division.

Step 3

Rank the candidates in the order of
your preference.

Step 4

Do not give the same ranking to
multiple candidates, and do not skip
rankings. If you make a mistake, take
your ‘spoiled’ ballot back to an
election official and ask for a new one.

Step 5

Once you have marked your ballot, fold your ballot paper in half, exit the booth, and drop it into  into the ballot box.
